## üéØ Habit Tracking Questions

### What types of habits can I track?

Habigrow supports four flexible habit types:

1. **Action-Based Habits** - Yes/No tracking (e.g., "Wake up early", "No sugar")
2. **Count-Based Habits** - Measurable quantities (e.g., "8 glasses of water", "10,000 steps")
3. **Timer-Based Habits** - Duration tracking (e.g., "30 min reading", "1 hour exercise")
4. **Checklist Habits** - Multi-step routines (e.g., morning routine, evening routine)

### How do I create a new habit?

1. Tap the "+" button
2. Enter habit name
3. Choose habit type (action, count, timer, or checklist)
4. Set schedule and reminders
5. Customize icon, color, and category
6. Save and start tracking

### Can I track habits multiple times per day?

Yes! You can set habits to track multiple times daily. For example:
- Drink water (8 times per day)
- Take breaks (every 2 hours)
- Practice gratitude (morning and evening)

### What happens if I miss a day?

Missing a day doesn't break your streak permanently. Habigrow offers:
- Streak recovery options
- Flexible tracking (you can mark as "skipped" or "missed")
- Focus on overall consistency, not perfection
- Analytics show completion rates, not just streaks

Goals in Habigrow:

- Link to daily habits for automatic progress

### Can I link habits to goals?

Absolutely! Linking habits to goals:
- Shows how daily actions contribute to objectives
- Automatically calculates goal progress

### What's the difference between habits and goals?

## üí° Tips & Best Practices

### How do I build habits successfully?

1. **Start small** - Begin with 2-3 habits, not 10+
2. **Be specific** - Clear, actionable habit definitions
3. **Set reminders** - Use notifications to build consistency
4. **Track honestly** - Accurate data = better insights
5. **Review weekly** - Check analytics and adjust
6. **Be patient** - Habits take 21-66 days to form
7. **Link to goals** - Connect habits to meaningful objectives

### What are common habit tracking mistakes?

Avoid these mistakes:
- ‚ùå Tracking too many habits at once
- ‚ùå Vague habit definitions
- ‚ùå Not setting reminders
- ‚ùå Giving up after missing days
- ‚ùå Ignoring analytics
- ‚ùå Not linking to goals

### How long does it take to form a habit?

Research shows:
- **21 days** - Minimum for simple habits
- **66 days** - Average for complex habits
- **Varies** - Depends on complexity and consistency

Focus on consistency, not perfection. Missing days is normal - what matters is getting back on track.
